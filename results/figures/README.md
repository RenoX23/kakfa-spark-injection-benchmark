## Honesty notes (carried from the data, not smoothed over)

- **Fig 4 shows no mean for network_degradation.** Its lead times are `0 s ×3, 65 s ×1`; a
  "mean 16.2 s" would misrepresent a 3-zeros-plus-one-outlier split as a warning window. The
  per-value breakdown is annotated instead — matching `baseline_thresholds.md` §5.
- **Fig 4 broker_kill is N/A, not 0 or 57 s.** For a binary up/down signal, threshold crossing
  *is* the outage; there is no distinct later crash event to lead into (recall 12.5%, n=1).
- **Figs 3–5 are the null-result story, not a win.** Fig 3/4 show the static baseline works for
  one class; Fig 5 shows the ML model does not beat a trivial baseline even at N=15. The only
  significant ML classification result (disk_pressure) is Fig 1, and §3.2 explains why it is
  not the early-warning result it superficially resembles.
